Mammals

The world's three great groups of mammals: monotremes, marsupials and placentals are all found in Australia. Monotremes are egg-laying mammals (nowadays found only in Australia and New Guinea). Marsupials have babies born in embryonic condition and kept firmly attached to a teat in a pouch or nestled behind a protective skin-flap while developing further. Placentals have unborn young nourished by a placenta (just to confuse things, some marsupials also have a placenta) and born at a more advanced stage, some still naked, blind and unable to walk for a week or two, others able to run on their day of birth. A total of 379 species of mammals have been recorded in Australia and surrounding continental waters; 357 indigenous and 22 introduced.
